The Birth of Planet Earth

A Cosmic Journey Through Time

The Cosmic Beginning: Solar Nebula and Supernova

About 4.6 billion years ago, a vast cloud of gas and dust, known as the Solar Nebula, floated through space. A nearby supernova created gravitational instability, leading to the formation of the Proto-Planetary Disk, where Earth began its journey.

Accretion: Building Blocks of Planets

The process of accretion explains how tiny particles in the solar nebula clumped together to form planetesimals and protoplanets, laying the groundwork for Earth and other celestial bodies.

The Hadean Eon: Earth's Hellish Start

During the Hadean Eon, Earth endured massive collisions and volcanic activity. Water from comets and asteroids helped stabilize the environment, setting the stage for life.

Layers of Earth: The Great Divide

Earth's layers formed due to gravity and heat, with heavy elements forming the core and lighter materials creating the crust. These layers drive geological phenomena like earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.

The Moon's Formation: A Giant Impact

The Giant Impact Hypothesis suggests that a Mars-sized protoplanet collided with Earth, forming the Moon. This event gave Earth its axial tilt and seasons while stabilizing its climate.

Earth’s Conditions for Life: A Perfect Balance

Volcanic activity, water, and a magnetic field contributed to Earth’s ability to support life, showcasing its finely tuned processes.
